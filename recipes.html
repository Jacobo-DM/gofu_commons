Gofu Recipes

The magic of Gofu begins here: tofu-making for every bean on Earth.

1. The Universal Gofu / Tofu Process

Gofu is tofu liberated. The process is ancient, simple, and globally adaptable:

  1. Soak your bean of choice until swollen (overnight preferred).
  2. Blend soaked beans with water to make a smooth slurry.
  3. Cook the slurry gently until it foams and thickens (this awakens the proteins).
  4. Strain through cloth to separate “bean milk” from the pulp.
  5. Coagulate the hot milk using an acid or mineral salt (lemon, vinegar, nigari, gypsum, or regional equivalents).
  6. Press the curds lightly or firmly depending on texture desired.
  7. Chill and cut.

This universal process becomes Gofu when you choose your bean — your land, your story, your flavor, your culture.

Black bean Gofu is purple-gray and silky. Chickpea Gofu is golden and custardy. Mung bean Gofu is pale, firm, and springy. White bean Gofu is creamy and mild. Fava Gofu is nutty and fragrant.

2. What Gofu Can Become

Because Gofu uses the ancestral tofu method, it inherits all of tofu’s possibilities — and then expands them with bean diversity.

  • Crispy Gofu croutons (pan-fried cubes)
  • Gofu cookies (yes, high-protein baking)
  • Gofu cutlets (pressed, sliced, seasoned, seared)
  • Gofu cream (for soups + sauces)
  • Gofu scramble (with turmeric, garlic, vegetables)
  • Gofu nuggets (breaded and baked)
  • Gofu sweets (custards, puddings, ice creams)
  • Gofu parchment (thin sheets for rolls or wraps)
  • Tempeh-style Gofu (fermented with bean variety of choice)

The world of tofu opens — but now with EVERY bean as a doorway.

3. Base Gofu (Example Method)

This is a general template; adapt per bean.

  • 2 cups dried beans (any)
  • Water (for soaking + blending + cooking)
  • 1–2 tbsp acid (lemon, vinegar) or mineral coagulant
  • Salt optional

Steps

  1. Soak beans overnight.
  2. Blend 1 part beans to ~3–4 parts water until smooth.
  3. Heat slurry and stir to prevent sticking.
  4. Strain through cloth to collect hot bean milk.
  5. Bring milk to a simmer; turn off heat.
  6. Add your coagulant slowly while stirring.
  7. Let curds form. Rest 10–15 minutes.
  8. Ladle curds into a cloth-lined mold; press lightly.
  9. Cool in water.

If the curds don’t set, add a bit more coagulant or heat gently. Every bean has its own story.

4. Gofu Variants

Family Gofu

Add aromatics, herbs, sautéed onions, garlic, or spices to the fresh curds before pressing.

Kudzu Gofu

Blend 1–2 tbsp kudzu/starch with cold water and stir into hot bean milk before pressing for firmer texture.

School & Community Gofu

  • Steam kettles or large pots
  • Immersion blenders
  • Easily seasoned
  • Students choose toppings

Gofu is scalable and democratic — ideal for large kitchens.
