Pan Dulce de la Olla

A warm holiday bread made with piloncillo spirit, gentle spices, and the magic of any bean milk you choose. Rooted in pan dulce traditions, open to every table.

Shared Bread
Low Cost
Bean-Friendly
Holiday Table

Why this bread?

Pan Dulce de la Olla honors the olla on the stove and the craft of baking in the hands. It offers celebration without extravagance: a feast made from almost nothing, rich in flavor, memory, and community.

Ingredients

For the syrup (la olla)

  • 1 cone piloncillo (or ¾ cup brown sugar + 1 tbsp molasses)
  • 1½ cups water
  • 1–2 cinnamon sticks
  • 2–3 cloves
  • Small piece orange peel (optional)
  • 1 tsp anise seeds or ½ tsp ground anise (optional)

For the dough

  • 3 to 3½ cups flour (all-purpose or bread)
  • 2¼ tsp active dry yeast (1 packet)
  • ½ cup Gofu milk, other bean milk, or cow’s milk (lukewarm)
  • 2 eggs (or 1 egg + 1 tbsp oil for affordability)
  • ¼ cup neutral oil or softened butter
  • ½ tsp salt

Optional add-ins

Raisins, chocolate pieces, toasted pumpkin seeds, citrus zest, dates, nuts, sesame seeds… let your own tradition finish the story.

Make the Olla Syrup

  1. In a pot, combine piloncillo, water, cinnamon, cloves, and orange peel.
  2. Simmer until the piloncillo dissolves into a dark, fragrant syrup.
  3. Remove from heat and let cool until warm. Strain out the spices.

Activate the Yeast

  1. Warm the Gofu/bean milk to body temperature.
  2. Stir in the yeast and let sit 8–10 minutes, until foamy.

Make the Dough

  1. In a large bowl, mix about 1 cup warm syrup, the yeast mixture, eggs (or egg + oil), and salt.
  2. Add flour a cup at a time until a soft dough forms.
  3. Knead 8–10 minutes, or 4 minutes then rest 5 and knead 2 more, until smooth and elastic.

First Rise

  1. Place the dough in a lightly oiled bowl, cover, and let rise 1–2 hours, until doubled.

Shape

  1. Shape into 1 large round, 2 smaller loaves, buns, braids, or festive shapes.
  2. Tuck in any add-ins (raisins, nuts, chocolate) as you shape.
  3. Brush lightly with syrup or milk and sprinkle sesame seeds if you like.

Second Rise & Bake

  1. Let rise again 30–45 minutes.
  2. Bake at 350°F / 175°C:
    • 25–30 minutes for buns
    • 35–45 minutes for loaves
  3. Bread is ready when golden and hollow-sounding when tapped. Brush with a little syrup for shine.
