Pan Dulce de la Olla

A simple, fragrant sweet bread made with piloncillo syrup, warm spices, and the option of Gofu or other bean-based milks. It honors pan dulce traditions while affirming the need for affordable, nourishing celebration in a time of rising costs and hunger.

Ingredients

For the syrup (la olla)

  • 1 cone piloncillo (or ¾ cup brown sugar + 1 tbsp molasses)
  • 1½ cups water
  • 1–2 cinnamon sticks
  • 2–3 cloves
  • Small piece orange peel (optional)
  • 1 tsp anise seeds or ½ tsp ground anise (optional)

For the dough

  • 3 to 3½ cups flour (all-purpose or bread)
  • 2¼ tsp active dry yeast (1 packet)
  • ½ cup Gofu milk, other bean milk, or cow’s milk (lukewarm)
  • 2 eggs (or 1 egg + 1 tbsp oil)
  • ¼ cup neutral oil or softened butter
  • ½ tsp salt

Optional add-ins

Raisins, chocolate, toasted pumpkin seeds, citrus zest, dates, nuts, sesame seeds… let your own tradition finish the story.

Make the Syrup

  1. In a pot, combine piloncillo, water, cinnamon, cloves, and orange peel.
  2. Simmer until the piloncillo dissolves into a dark, fragrant syrup.
  3. Remove from heat and let cool until warm. Strain out the spices.

Activate the Yeast

  1. Warm the Gofu/bean milk to body temperature.
  2. Stir in the yeast and let sit 8–10 minutes, until foamy.

Make the Dough

  1. In a large bowl, mix 1 cup warm syrup, the yeast mixture, eggs (or egg + oil), and salt.
  2. Add flour a cup at a time until a soft dough forms.
