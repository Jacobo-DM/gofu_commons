The Gofu Commons Declaration

This declaration releases Gofu as a shared pattern of nourishment, not a commodity.

1. No Ownership over Gofu

Gofu is a simple method for transforming beans, water, and time into a nourishing, adaptable base food. No person, company, organization, or government may claim exclusive ownership, trademark, copyright, or patent protection over:

  • the name “Gofu” as it relates to this food and practice;
  • the core method of soaking, cooking, and transforming beans into Gofu;
  • any minor variations that leave the core process essentially the same.

2. Right to Cook, Share, and Adapt

Any person, family, kitchen, or community may:

  • cook Gofu for themselves and others;
  • teach others how to cook Gofu, with or without payment for their time;
  • adapt Gofu using local beans, ingredients, spices, and cultural foodways;
  • share written, audio, or visual guides for making Gofu.

No permission is required to cook, teach, or adapt Gofu, and no one may be charged a fee simply for using the word “Gofu” or following the core process.

3. Commons, Not Commodity

Gofu may absolutely appear in restaurants, school cafeterias, packaged meals, and other economic spaces — but the underlying pattern remains part of the commons. No entity may:

  • prevent others from serving Gofu or teaching the method;
  • create barriers that would stop mutual aid kitchens, shelters, or communities from using Gofu freely;
  • claim that Gofu itself is proprietary technology.

4. Teaching Gofu

Teaching Gofu is itself an act of commons-building. Lessons, workshops, Discovery Mode modules, and Monster Kitchen stories related to Gofu may be:

  • freely shared;
  • adapted or translated;
  • integrated into existing programs and curricula.

Educators and facilitators may be compensated for their time and skill, but no paywall may be placed on the right to cook or learn the core Gofu process.

5. Spirit of the Declaration

No one is obligated to attribute or reference this document when making Gofu. This declaration is an invitation, not a contract. Those who resonate with it are simply asked to carry forward its spirit:

  • food as a right,
  • nourishment as relationship,
  • simplicity as strength.
Gofu is offered openly, joyfully, and irreversibly to the world.
May it travel far.
May it feed many.
May its simplicity strengthen the hands that need it most.
