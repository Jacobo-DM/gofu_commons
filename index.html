Gofu, Offered to the Commons

A simple bean-based food, released freely for anyone to cook, teach, and adapt.

Gofu is not a brand. It is a shared pattern for nourishment that anyone can carry: families, mutual aid kitchens, shelters, school cafeterias, street corners, and places we haven’t imagined yet.

No patents No trademarks Food as a right Teach & remix

What this Commons Means

By the Gofu Commons declaration:

  • No individual, organization, company, or government may claim ownership, exclusivity, trademark, copyright, or patent protection over Gofu or its core processes.
  • Communities are encouraged to adapt Gofu using local beans, flavors, and cultural foodways of their region.
  • Gofu may be used anywhere nourishment and sovereignty are needed: shelters, school cafeterias, mutual aid kitchens, home cooking, regenerative agriculture programs, and more.
  • Teaching Gofu is part of the commons: lessons, workshops, Discovery Mode modules, and Monster Kitchen stories may all be freely created and shared.
  • No one is obliged to attribute this declaration, but all are invited to carry its spirit: food as a right, not a commodity; nourishment as relationship, not extraction.
Gofu is offered openly, joyfully, and irreversibly to the world.
May it travel far.
May it feed many.
May its simplicity strengthen the hands that need it most.
