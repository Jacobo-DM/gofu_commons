Gofu Commons Declaration

What Gofu Is

Gofu is an open-source, bean-based food created from simple methods of soaking, grinding, heating, curdling, and pressing legumes. It can be made anywhere in the world using local beans — chickpea, pinto, lentil, mung, black bean, fava, or whatever grows nearby.

Its purpose is to expand food sovereignty, reduce reliance on monocultures, and support communities with a low-cost, locally adaptable plant protein.

Why the Commons?

Gofu is not a product or a brand. It is a shared food technology offered to humanity. By placing Gofu in the public domain, we remove as many barriers as we can: no patents, no trademarks, no ownership claims — only the invitation to cook, adapt, and teach.

Declaration (Summary)

By this declaration:

  • No individual, organization, company, or government may claim ownership, exclusivity, trademark, copyright, or patent protection over Gofu or its core processes. It belongs to everyone.
  • Communities are encouraged to adapt Gofu using local beans, flavors, and cultural foodways of their region.
  • Gofu may be used in shelters, school cafeterias, mutual aid kitchens, home cooking, regenerative agriculture programs — anywhere nourishment and sovereignty are needed.
  • Teaching Gofu is itself part of the commons: lessons, workshops, Discovery Mode modules, and Monster Kitchen stories may all be freely created and shared.
  • No one is obligated to attribute this declaration, but all are invited to carry its spirit: food as a right, not a commodity; nourishment as relationship, not extraction.

Gofu is offered openly, joyfully, and irreversibly to the world.
May it travel far.
May it feed many.
May its simplicity strengthen the hands that need it most.
