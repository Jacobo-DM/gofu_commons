What is Gofu?

Gofu is a gentle, adaptable food: a tofu-like base made from beans other than soy. It’s designed for home kitchens, school kitchens, and community kitchens that want affordable nourishment without relying on a single crop.

Core idea: a simple method that anyone can learn and pass on, so that more communities can turn dry beans and water into fresh, everyday protein.

A small origin story

My mom loves chicken salad. I wanted to make a healthier version, so I thought of tofu as a substitute. Then I remembered how much estrogen is associated with soybeans, and I wondered:

What if we swap out that bean for a different one—something gentler and more flexible for everyday eating?

That question stayed with me. I started experimenting with other beans, a pot of water, and whatever tools we already had at home. Somewhere between those small experiments and my mom’s lunch, Gofu was born.

It’s a simple story, but it carries the warmth that Gofu deserves: one family kitchen, one loved person, and a wish for food that is kinder to bodies and to the places we call home.
