Gofu Commons

A simple, adaptable, tofu-style protein made from many beans, seeds, and regional crops — not just soy.

Food systems are under strain: fertilizer dependence, fragile distribution, monocultures, rising costs, and ecological damage. Gofu is a small, practical response — something humble that home kitchens, school kitchens, and community kitchens can learn, remake, and pass on.

What is Gofu?

Gofu is a bean-based food inspired by tofu, but freed from reliance on a single crop. It can be made from chickpeas, lentils, peas, fava beans, black-eyed peas, and other regionally available legumes — and potentially from seeds and future adaptations as the commons grows.

The core idea is simple: dry beans, water, heat, and shared technique can become fresh, everyday protein almost anywhere.

A small origin story

Gofu began in a family kitchen. I wanted to make a healthier version of my mom’s beloved chicken salad and started wondering what would happen if tofu-tech were opened up beyond soybeans.

That question led to small experiments: beans, water, simple tools, and the hope that something nourishing, affordable, and more ecologically flexible might emerge. Gofu was born from that wish — not as a product, but as a food commons.

Why this matters

  • It reduces dependence on a single global crop.
  • It welcomes local adaptation based on place, climate, and culture.
  • It is accessible to ordinary kitchens with simple tools.
  • It supports community resilience, experimentation, and shared knowledge.
  • It opens a path toward fermentation, preservation, and deeper food craft.

The spirit of the project

Gofu Commons is meant to be copied, tested, improved, translated, remixed, and shared. This is not a closed recipe and not a branded food product. It is a commons seed.

If you make it, adapt it, or teach it, please pass the generosity forward.

All text, recipes, and materials in this repository are dedicated to the public domain under CC0 1.0 Universal.
